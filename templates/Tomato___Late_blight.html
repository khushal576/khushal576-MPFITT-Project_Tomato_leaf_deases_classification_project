What is it?

Tomato Late Blight is a fungal disease caused by the oomycete Phytophthora infestans. It manifests as large, dark, irregularly shaped lesions on the leaves, often with a fuzzy white mold on the underside. The disease can spread quickly and devastate entire tomato crops, particularly in humid conditions.

Why does it occur?

Tomato Late Blight thrives in cool, moist conditions, particularly during periods of high humidity or rainfall. The fungus can survive in plant debris and soil and spreads rapidly through wind, rain, or irrigation water.

Prevention:

  • Plant Resistant Varieties: Choose tomato varieties that are resistant to late blight if available in your area.
  • Crop Rotation: Rotate tomatoes with unrelated crops to reduce the buildup of fungal spores in the soil.
  • Avoid Overhead Watering: Water the plants at the base to avoid wetting the foliage, which can help prevent the spread of spores.
  • Remove Infected Plant Debris: Clean up and remove any infected plant debris from the garden to reduce the source of fungal spores.
  • Improve Air Circulation: Proper spacing between plants and adequate ventilation can help reduce humidity and minimize fungal growth.
  • Apply Fungicides Preventatively: Apply fungicides containing chlorothalonil or copper before symptoms appear, especially during periods of high disease pressure.

Treatment:

While there's no cure for Tomato Late Blight, you can take steps to manage the disease:

  • Remove Infected Leaves: Prune and remove infected leaves to prevent the spread of the disease.
  • Apply Fungicides: Fungicides containing chlorothalonil or copper can help manage late blight if applied early and regularly. Follow label instructions carefully.
  • Reduce Leaf Wetness: Avoid overhead watering and irrigate early in the day to allow foliage to dry quickly.
  • Improve Air Circulation: Proper spacing between plants and pruning to increase airflow can help reduce humidity and minimize fungal growth.
  • Monitor Regularly: Regularly inspect tomato plants for signs of late blight and take prompt action if symptoms appear.
